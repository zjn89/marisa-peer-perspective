## 4. CORE IDEAS -- REPEATED >=3x ACROSS CORPUS (Likely True Beliefs)

These ideas appear consistently across Peer's books, talks, blog posts, and RTT training materials -- not one-off remarks.

### 4.1 "I Am Enough" -- The Ur-Belief
- **Frequency:** Appears in every book (2018 onward), every talk, all marketing, the RTT training, the mirror exercise.
- **Formulation:** Most human suffering stems from the childhood-formed belief "I am not enough" -- not smart enough, attractive enough, wealthy enough, lovable enough.
- **The Four Hazardous Words:** "I am not enough" is the root of most psychological problems.
- **Antidote:** Write "I Am Enough" on your mirror; repeat as mantra; embed through repetition until the mind accepts it.

### 4.2 The Mind Responds to Words and Pictures (Literally)
- **Frequency:** Every book, every talk, core to RTT.
- **Formulation:** Every thought and word is a "blueprint" that the mind and body must work to make real. The mind does not judge whether the blueprint is good/bad/true/false -- it simply accepts and acts.
- **Consequence:** If you say "this job is killing me," your body starts producing stress chemicals. If you say "I am dreading this," your mind may give you a stomachache to help you avoid it.

### 4.3 The Mind Works Only in the Present Tense
- **Frequency:** All books, RTT training, every talk.
- **Formulation:** You cannot say "I will be confident." The mind only responds to present-tense statements: "I am confident now." Words like "will," "later," "tomorrow," "maybe" are invisible to the subconscious.

### 4.4 The Mind Loves the Familiar (and Resists the Unfamiliar)
- **Frequency:** All books, RTT training, TEDx talks.
- **Formulation:** Evolutionary holdover from tribal days when unfamiliar = danger/death. Explains why people stay in bad relationships, dead-end jobs, destructive habits. Change requires making "the unfamiliar familiar" through repetition.

### 4.5 Childhood Programming (0-7 Years) Creates Adult Operating System
- **Frequency:** Tell Yourself a Better Lie, Ultimate Confidence, I Am Enough, blog, all RTT training, Your Mind Your Rules.
- **Formulation:** Core beliefs form before age 7. Three unmet childhood needs (safe, loved, significant) morph into fixed adult limiting beliefs. RTT works by accessing these through hypnosis and reframing them.

### 4.6 Imagination is More Powerful than Knowledge (When Dealing with the Mind)
- **Frequency:** Multiple books, RTT training, talks.
- **Formulation:** In a battle between emotion/will/imagination and logic, the former always wins. If you vividly imagine your partner cheating, you'll feel the emotional reaction as though real. Therefore, deliberate visualization is the primary tool for change.

### 4.7 Negative/Neutral Qualifiers Are Invisible to the Subconscious
- **Frequency:** All books, all talks, RTT training.
- **Formulation:** Words like "don't," "can't," "no," "not" are not registered by the subconscious. "I don't want to be anxious" creates a picture of anxiety. The mind delivers what you picture, not what you negate.

### 4.8 The Placebo/Nocebo Principle as Central Mechanism
- **Frequency:** All books, especially You Can Be Younger, Tell Yourself a Better Lie.
- **Formulation:** What you believe about your body, your capabilities, your future -- the mind makes real. Belief is the most powerful drug.

### 4.9 Self-Talk as Literal Command
- **Frequency:** All books, all talks.
- **Formulation:** "Your mind's job is to do what you tell it to do. Your mind does specifically what it thinks you want it to do." The strongest human drive is to act in a way that consistently matches our thinking. Behaviour always aligns with deepest beliefs.

### 4.10 Anger as Secondary Emotion
- **Frequency:** Tell Yourself a Better Lie, multiple blog posts, podcast appearances.
- **Formulation:** Anger always masks deeper emotions -- hurt, sadness, fear. Suppressed anger manifests as depression, anxiety, physical symptoms.

## 5. SELF-CREATED TERMINOLOGY & CONCEPTS

### 5.1 RTT (Rapid Transformational Therapy) -- [Registered Trademark]
- **Definition:** A hybrid therapeutic approach combining hypnotherapy (regression + hypnotic conditioning), CBT, NLP, psychotherapy, and neuroscience/neuroplasticity. Claims to produce results in 1-3 sessions.
- **Origin:** Created by Marisa Peer. Launched as a formal school in 2015.
- **Status:** Registered trademark (R). Not recognized by mainstream psychological or medical bodies. Endorsed by IICT (International Institute of Complementary Therapies -- a registration body, not an accrediting body; accepts practitioners in "Quantum Photo-Somatics" and "Tibetan Pulsing").
- **Sources:** [1P] marisapeer.com; RTT training materials; [2P] hypnotherapy-directory.org.uk

### 5.2 Command Cell Therapy (aka Cell Regeneration Therapy, RTT Cell Command Therapy)
- **Definition:** A technique where the practitioner (or individual through guided audio) speaks directly to the body's cells in a hypnotic state, commanding them to heal, regenerate, and function optimally.
- **Framework:** D.I.C.C.C. -- Direct, Instruct, Command, Compel, Code (the fifth "C" varies between sources as "Code" or "Compel").
- **Origin:** Introduced in Forever Young (1997) as "cell regeneration therapy," formalized within RTT.
- **Peer's Personal Claim:** Used to heal herself from cancer twice, regrow bone, and heal wounds.
- **Scientific Anchoring:** Peer frames this as applied neuroplasticity. However, the claim that thoughts can "command" cells to heal extends well beyond what conventional neuroscience supports.
- **Sources:** [1P] Forever Young / You Can Be Younger; [2P] bengreenfieldlife.com podcast; [3P] practitioner descriptions

### 5.3 "I Am Enough" -- Movement / Brand
- **Definition:** Both a mantra and a branded movement. The core belief that "I am enough" (and have always been enough) is the single most transformative shift a person can make.
- **Key Practice:** Write "I Am Enough" on mirrors, fridges, everywhere visible.
- **Sources:** [1P] marisapeer.com/iamenoughcourse/ ; book I Am Enough (2018)

### 5.4 The "3 Rules of the Mind" (Also Expanded as 12, 14, or 16 Rules)
- **Core 3 (most frequently cited):**
  1. Your mind always does what it thinks you want it to do
  2. Your mind moves you away from pain and towards pleasure (and loves the familiar)
  3. Your mind responds to the pictures you make and the words you say
- **Expanded List (up to 16 rules):** See Appendix A. Peer teaches these in RTT and in her newest book "Your Mind, Your Rules" (2026).
- **Co-Creator Attribution:** Co-developed with Gil Boyne (per some sources)
- **Sources:** [1P] marisapeer.com/how-to-control-your-thoughts-rules-of-the-mind/ ; [1P] Your Mind, Your Rules (2026); [2P] mindvalley.com

### 5.5 DICCC Method (or D.I.C.C.C.)
- **Definition:** The mnemonic framework for giving precise instructions to the mind and body during Command Cell Therapy.
- **Breakdown:** Direct, Instruct, Command, Compel, and Code
- **Note:** Inconsistency in whether 5th C is "Code" or "Compel" (some sources list only 4 steps and fold "Code" into "Command").
- **Sources:** [2P] RTT practitioner descriptions; [3P] various blog summaries

### 5.6 RFPI (Role, Function, Purpose, Intention)
- **Definition:** A technique to help clients understand the unconscious emotional purpose behind their behaviors or symptoms.
- **Origin:** Formalized in Tell Yourself a Better Lie (2022).
- **Sources:** [1P] Tell Yourself a Better Lie; [2P] sobrief.com summary

### 5.7 "Upgrading the Child" / "Installing the Cheerleader" / "Dialogue with the Hurter"
- **Definition:** Specific RTT techniques for inner child work, replacing the inner critic, and imagined dialogues for emotional release.
- **Origin:** Formalized in Tell Yourself a Better Lie (2022).
- **Sources:** [1P] Tell Yourself a Better Lie

### 5.8 "The Three Ages"
- **Definition:** Chronological age (birth certificate), biological age (organs), and the age you feel (most important -- the age of your organs correlates with the age you feel).
- **Origin:** You Can Be Younger (2014).
- **Sources:** [1P] You Can Be Younger

### 5.9 "The 3 Rs Eating Rule"
- **Definition:** Eat only foods that Grow/Roam, can be eaten Raw, or you Recognize as natural food.
- **Origin:** You Can Be Thin (2008).
- **Sources:** [1P] You Can Be Thin

### 5.10 Six Types of Overeaters
- **Definition:** Addictive, Emotional, Habitual, Ignorant, Destructive/Angry -- a diagnostic taxonomy for identifying the psychological root of overeating.
- **Origin:** You Can Be Thin (2008).
- **Sources:** [1P] You Can Be Thin

### 5.11 Five Core Human Needs
- **Definition:** Acceptance/Belonging, Security/Certainty, Significance/Importance, Growth/Development, Contribution/Purpose.
- **Origin:** Ultimate Confidence (2009).
- **Note:** This framework shares obvious DNA with Maslow's Hierarchy and with Tony Robbins' "6 Human Needs" (Certainty, Variety, Significance, Connection/Love, Growth, Contribution). Peer's version drops "Variety" and "Connection/Love" and adds "Acceptance/Belonging." It is unclear whether this is direct borrowing or parallel development.
- **Sources:** [1P] Ultimate Confidence; [2P] sobrief.com

### 5.12 "Neurobics"
- **Definition:** Doing familiar tasks in novel ways (e.g., using non-dominant hand) to keep neurons firing and brain young.
- **Origin:** You Can Be Younger (2014).
- **Note:** Term may predate Peer; appears in broader brain-training literature.
- **Sources:** [1P] You Can Be Younger

### 5.13 "The Four Hazardous Words"
- **Definition:** The words "I am not enough" are identified as literally hazardous to physical and mental health.
- **Origin:** Public talks, blog, I Am Enough book.
- **Sources:** [1P] marisapeer.com; [2P] wvnews.com columns referencing her work

## 7. CONTRADICTIONS, TENSIONS & CRITICAL OBSERVATIONS

### 7.1 Practitioner Count Discrepancy
- **Claim A:** "Over 17,000 RTT practitioners trained" (some speaker profiles)
- **Claim B:** "Over 18,000 RTT practitioners trained" (other profiles)
- **Claim C:** "Over 177,000 RTT practitioners" (the most dramatic claim, circulated in some marketing)
- **Analysis:** These figures cannot all be true simultaneously. The jump from 18K to 177K suggests either a data entry error (extra digit), or counting includes free course enrollments rather than certified practitioners, or the figure varies dramatically by source quality.
- **Resolution:** Unresolved. No audited/verified count available.

### 7.2 Scientific Evidence vs. Marketing Language
- **Claim:** RTT is "award-winning," "backed by science," draws on "neuroscience and neuroplasticity."
- **Reality (per Psychology StackExchange investigation, 2020):** RTT's own lawyer confirmed "no current published papers on RTT." Feasibility studies were announced as "starting soon" but no peer-reviewed results have appeared as of 2026. RTT is endorsed by IICT (not a scientific body; accepts practitioners in pseudoscientific modalities for a ~$179 annual registration fee).
- **Assessment:** Claims of "30+ years of research" appear to refer to anecdotal clinical experience, not controlled studies. The neuroscience framing borrows scientific language without scientific validation.
- **Source:** [2P] psychology.stackexchange.com/questions/25819

### 7.3 "Britain's Best Therapist" -- Verifiability
- Claimed everywhere (marketing, book covers, speaker profiles) as awarded by Tatler magazine.
- No specific issue date or year was found in search. This may be a genuine award but is treated as fact without citation in virtually every source.
- This is common in the speaker/self-help industry but should be noted.

### 7.4 RTT's Scope of Claims
- RTT is marketed as effective for: depression, anxiety, phobias, eating disorders, addictions, OCD, acne, allergies, chronic illness, career issues, relationship problems, physical healing, weight loss, and aging.
- **Tension:** No single therapeutic modality has been demonstrated effective for everything. The breadth of claims itself is a common red flag in evaluating therapeutic approaches.
- **Placebo Assessment:** Multiple Psychology StackExchange commenters suggest RTT's effects are likely placebo-equivalent, given the lack of controlled trials and the known placebo-level effects of hypnosis in some studies.

### 7.5 NLP Incorporation
- RTT explicitly incorporates NLP. NLP is widely regarded as pseudoscientific by the mainstream psychological community.
- Peer does not address or acknowledge this controversy in her public-facing materials.

### 7.6 "Command Cell Therapy" vs. Medical Reality
- Peer claims to have used Command Cell Therapy to heal herself from cancer twice and regrow bone.
- These are extraordinary medical claims. No medical documentation has been provided publicly. Peer does not claim to be a medical doctor.
- The technique is always caveated as "complementary to (not replacement for) medical treatment," but the marketing tells a different story.

### 7.7 Family Relationship Inconsistency
