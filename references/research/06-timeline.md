## PHASE 0: ORIGINS & FORMATIVE YEARS (1957–1981)

### Birth & Family

- **[CONFIRMED] 1957-04-12** — Born in Newcastle, United Kingdom. Full name: Marisa Peer.
- **[CONFIRMED]** Father: Ronald Peer, a distinguished headmaster (school principal). Described as emotionally absent, devoted to "everyone else's children."
- **[CONFIRMED]** Mother: Dee Sadler (also listed as Dee Saddler). Described as beautiful but "completely unfulfilled," frequently ill. Attempted suicide multiple times during Marisa's childhood (throwing herself down stairs).
- **[CONFIRMED]** Middle child. Siblings: at least one brother and younger sister Sian Peer. Niece: Carlyss Peer (actress/narrator).
- **[CONFIRMED]** Grandmother: Marisa credits her as the single most important person in her childhood — "the one person who believed in me." She has stated: "You need one person in your life to believe in you. If you have that one person, you'll be okay."
- **[CONFIRMED]** Childhood experience: Outwardly privileged (nice house, "white picket fence") but internally lonely. Felt overlooked, "not attractive, not smart, not good enough." This feeling of "not-enoughness" later became the core insight behind her entire therapeutic philosophy.
- **[OBSERVATION]** Marisa observed that in her family, each member adopted a specific role: mother = "the sick one," father = "the brilliant one," brother = "the brilliant one," younger sister = "the cute pretty one," Marisa = "the caring one." This insight — that children adopt one of four survival roles (sick, brilliant, caring, rebellious) — became foundational to her therapeutic model.

### Education

- **[CONFIRMED]** Melbourn Village College
- **[CONFIRMED]** Cambridge College of Arts of Technology
- **[CONFIRMED]** Studied in London to become a child psychologist
- **[APPROXIMATE] c. 1977–1978 (Age 20)** — Told by doctors she could not have children. Developed an eating disorder and health challenges during this period.

> **THOUGHT EVOLUTION NOTE:** The childhood pattern of "not-enoughness" + being told she could not conceive + developing an eating disorder created the lived-experience foundation for her later work on self-worth, body image, and fertility. She would later say: "I know what it feels like to believe you are broken."

## PHASE 1: LOS ANGELES & THE PIVOT TO HYPNOTHERAPY (1982–1986)

### The Jane Fonda Years

- **[APPROXIMATE] c. 1982 (Age 25)** — Moved to Los Angeles, USA. Became a personal trainer at **Jane Fonda's Fitness Center** on Robertson Boulevard.
- **[CONFIRMED]** While training clients, she became deeply concerned that many women attending the studio struggled with eating disorders, body dysmorphia, and compulsive exercise — issues that physical training alone could not resolve.

> **TURNING POINT #1:** The realization that physical fitness does not fix psychological pain. This gap drove her to seek deeper tools — leading her to hypnotherapy.

### Hypnotherapy Training Under Gil Boyne

- **[CONFIRMED] 1985-08 to 1988-09** — Completed advanced certification in Hypnotherapy at the **Hypnotism Training Institute of Los Angeles**, studying directly under **Gil Boyne** (1924–2010), founder of the American Council of Hypnotist Examiners (A.C.H.E.) and a legendary figure in modern hypnotherapy.
- **[CONFIRMED]** Gil Boyne famously said of Marisa: *"Marisa is without doubt one of a few women in history to have had a profound impact on the field of hypnotherapy and psychotherapy. She began moulding the field of therapy into something much greater than it had ever been."*
- **[CONFIRMED]** The "Rules of the Mind" — the foundational principles of RTT — were developed jointly by Marisa Peer and Gil Boyne.
- **[CONFIRMED]** Additional studies at: Proudfoot School of Hypnotherapy and Psychotherapy; Atkinson Ball College of Hypnotherapy and Hypnohealing; Pritikin Longevity Center (anti-aging, health, long-term wellness). Supplementary studies in hypno-healing, medical hypnotherapy, and Gestalt Analysis.

### Return to London

- **[APPROXIMATE] c. 1986** — Returned to London, England, and began building a private therapy practice.

> **THOUGHT EVOLUTION NOTE:** Boyne's integration of Gestalt therapy with hypnosis, and his emphasis on rapid, results-focused intervention, directly shaped Peer's later development of RTT. She took his analytical hypnotherapy / regression techniques and expanded them into a multi-modality framework.

## PHASE 4: THE "I AM ENOUGH" REVOLUTION & RTT CREATION (2013–2017)

### The Core Insight

Through decades of clinical observation, Marisa identified a pattern: across all presenting issues (addiction, self-sabotage, relationship dysfunction, eating disorders, career stagnation), the common underlying belief was "I am not enough" — not good enough, not worthy enough, not lovable enough.

> **TURNING POINT #2:** The crystallization of "not-enoughness" as the root cause of most psychological suffering. This shifted her practice from treating surface symptoms to systematically targeting the core identity belief. This became the intellectual and emotional heart of her entire methodology.

### The "I Am Enough" Movement

- **[CONFIRMED] 2013** — Launched the **"I Am Enough" Movement**, centered on building inner confidence and self-esteem by changing the core identity narrative. People worldwide began marking their mirrors with the words "I AM ENOUGH" as a daily affirmation.
- **[CONFIRMED]** By 2024, the movement had reached **over 2,000 schools worldwide** and inspired the International Day of the Girl Child Inspiration Award for the #MarkYourMirror campaign.

### TEDx & Major Speaking

- **[CONFIRMED] 2014-06-30** — Delivered TEDx talk **"To Reach Beyond Your Limits by Training Your Mind"** at TEDxKCS (King's College School, London). This became her most viral talk, accumulating millions of views. Core message: the four rules of how the mind works — (1) your mind does what it thinks you want; (2) it moves toward pleasure and away from pain; (3) feelings are shaped by internal pictures and self-talk; (4) the mind loves the familiar.
- **[APPROXIMATE] c. 2014–2015** — Second TEDx talk: **"How to Avoid Rejection and Get Connection"** at TEDxGoodenoughCollege (London).

- **[CONFIRMED] 2014-04-03** — Published ***You Can Be Younger: Use the Power of Your Mind to Look and Feel 10 Years Younger in 10 Simple Steps*** (Piatkus / Hachette UK).

### RTT Founding

- **[CONFIRMED] 2015** — Founded the **RTT School** and formalized **Rapid Transformational Therapy (RTT)** as a distinct methodology. After 30+ years of clinical practice and repeated requests from other therapists to teach her methods, she finally agreed to systematize and share her approach.
- **[CONFIRMED]** RTT integrates: Hypnotherapy + Psychotherapy + NLP + CBT + Neuroscience + Neuroplasticity principles.
- **[CONFIRMED]** Core RTT distinctives: aims to go directly to root cause (not surface symptoms); designed to deliver lasting transformation, often in 1–3 sessions; uses the "Rules of the Mind" as its foundational framework.
- **[CONFIRMED] 2015** — Voted **Best Speaker at Awesomeness Fest** (second time winning this honor at A-Fest).

> **TURNING POINT #3:** The decision to shift from one-to-one therapy to scalable training. This transformed Marisa from elite therapist to global movement-builder. She acknowledged initial reluctance to train others, fearing dilution of quality — but the demand (and results) proved overwhelming.

### Awards & Recognition

- **[CONFIRMED] 2017** — Received **CPD Training Provider of Excellence** (Lifetime Achievement).
- **[CONFIRMED]** Awarded **Fellowship by the National Council of Psychotherapists** for contributions to hypnotherapy.
